Every Era, Every Mood

This is my musical journey, organized by era. Each phase shaped how I think, create, and see the world. Plug in your headphones and explore.

origins

The Foundation: Jazz & Classics

2015 & Before

Before everything else, there was jazz. Ella Fitzgerald's voice was the first music that made me stop and listen — really listen. Frankie Valli taught me that heartbreak could sound beautiful. And "Take Five" by Dave Brubeck? That song broke every rule I thought music had, and made me realize that's exactly what made it great.

Featured Artists

Ella Fitzgerald, Frankie Valli, Dave Brubeck, Billie Holiday, Nina Simone

Playlist Vibe

Velvet vocals, smoky jazz clubs, black-and-white elegance

volume up

The Rebellion: 70s & 80s Rock

The Guitar Era

This is the era of big feelings and bigger guitar solos. I learned about dynamics here — how to build tension, when to hold back, and how a perfectly timed pause can hit harder than any note. My driving playlist still lives in this decade.

Featured Artists

Fleetwood Mac, Heart, Led Zeppelin, The Eagles, Queen

Playlist Vibe

Windows down, road trips, screaming lyrics you definitely don't know all the words to

still on repeat

Home Base: 90s Rock

My Forever Genre

There's something about 90s rock that never gets old. Maybe it's the honesty — the lyrics that actually mean something, the rawness of the production, the feeling that the artists were making music for themselves first. This era shaped my taste more than any other, and I don't think I'll ever outgrow it.

This playlist is basically my personality in song form. If you want to understand me, start here.

Featured Artists

Matchbox 20, Green Day, The Pixies, Oasis, Third Eye Blind, Counting Crows

Playlist Vibe

Nostalgic but not dated, angsty in a comforting way, songs you know every word to

new sounds

The Expansion: Pop, R&B & Hip-Hop

Genre-Hopping

Somewhere along the way, I stopped limiting myself. Brent Faiyaz's R&B is smooth in a way that makes everything else feel rushed. SZA writes lyrics that feel like they were pulled from my own brain. Kendrick Lamar makes me think. J. Cole makes me feel. And Drake? Drake is just...everywhere, and I'm okay with that.

Featured Artists

Brent Faiyaz, SZA, Drake, Kendrick Lamar, J. Cole, The Weeknd

Playlist Vibe

Late nights, neon lights, feelings you can't quite name

on repeat

Right Now: The Current Mix

The Now

Right now, my taste is a beautiful mess. Laufey is bringing jazz back in a way that feels fresh. Lizzy McAlpine writes lyrics that make me want to stare out a window dramatically. Coldplay is still Coldplay (and I'm still not sorry about loving them). Billie Holiday is in the rotation too, because some things are timeless. This is where I am now — a little bit of everything, all at once.

Featured Artists

Laufey, Lizzy McAlpine, Coldplay, Billie Holiday, Phoebe Bridgers, Hozier

Playlist Vibe

Cozy evenings, gentle melancholy, songs for thinking

What's Next

The evolution never stops. There's always a new artist to discover, an old favorite to rediscover, a song that hits different at 2 AM than it did at noon. If you have recommendations, I'm always listening.
